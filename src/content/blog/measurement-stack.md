Network failures and censorship signals are more than technical anomalies. They reveal which actors control access, where accountability sits, and how infrastructure decisions shape public life. Measurement turns those events into evidence that can be discussed, documented, and challenged.

## The stack

- Compare DNS behavior across resolver classes.
- Validate HTTP responses with regional probes.
- Track routing baselines to detect upstream interference.

## Why it matters

Layered evidence separates governance-driven interference from ordinary failure and builds a clearer picture of infrastructure power.
